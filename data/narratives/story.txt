Title: The 12:01 Echo

The dust in the workshop tasted like copper and old paper. Dr. Aris shifted her flashlight, the beam landing on a silver pocket watch hanging from a single silk thread.

"Is it ticking?" her assistant whispered, his voice trembling at 75 decibels in the silent room.

Aris didn't answer. She reached out. The watch face didn't have numbers; it had names. Thorne. Aris. Oakhaven. At the center, the hands weren't moving, but the glass was warm to the touch.

"It’s not ticking," Aris finally said, her fingers brushing the cold silver. "It’s... breathing."

Suddenly, the flashlight flickered. The date on her digital watch jumped from January 9th to August 14th, then went blank. They weren't alone in 1892, and they certainly weren't alone now
